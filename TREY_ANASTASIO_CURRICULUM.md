# Trey Anastasio Vocabulary Curriculum

## Scope

This curriculum teaches recurring Trey Anastasio-style guitar vocabulary through original studies. It does not copy or transcribe copyrighted solos. The practical target is to build the habits that make Trey lines recognizable: pattern fluency, harmony-first note choice, rhythmic displacement, controlled dissonance, patient peaks, and singing sustain.

## Research Findings

- VERIFIED: Trey described learning scale navigation from Jerry Garcia by walking diatonically through scale shapes, including groups of four, and then generalizing patterns into other scales and modes. Source: https://www.guitarworld.com/lessons/phish-scales-trey-anastasio-breaks-down-his-improvisation-techniques
- VERIFIED: Trey described rhythmic variation as a major part of his improvising, while composition leans heavily into harmony. Source: https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2025/04/21/after-forty-years-phish-isnt-seeking-resolution
- VERIFIED: Trey described using constraints, imagery, key, and intensity parameters as generative limits for improvisation. Source: https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2025/04/21/after-forty-years-phish-isnt-seeking-resolution
- VERIFIED: "You Enjoy Myself" is a central composed-plus-jam vehicle, with a composed opening, tramps/funk section, jam segment, bass/drums, and vocal jam. Source: https://phish.net/song/you-enjoy-myself/history
- VERIFIED: "Stash" is framed around jazz influence, ii-V-I gravity, extended dominant tension, and release. Source: https://phish.net/song/stash/history
- VERIFIED: "Reba" pairs a difficult composed section with an improvised jam often discussed through tension and release. Source: https://phish.net/song/reba/history
- VERIFIED: "Divided Sky" is a melodic composition with a famous pause/release behavior and a recognizable instrumental hook. Source: https://phish.net/song/divided-sky/history
- VERIFIED: Recent rig reporting lists two Analogman-modded Tube Screamers, a Ross Compressor, Whammy, wah, delay, Boomerang, vibe, amps, and Languedoc cabinets in Trey's 2024 live rig context. Source: https://treysguitarrig.com/2024/08/06/2024-summer/
- INFERRED: For a student, the best "top licks" curriculum is not a list of copied phrases; it is a set of reusable cells that map to the published evidence above and can be transposed, varied rhythmically, and resolved musically.

## Top Vocabulary Devices

1. Diatonic groups of four
   - Source basis: Trey explicitly discussed this pattern family.
   - Skill: clean alternate picking, scale-degree awareness, controlled starts from non-root degrees.
   - App card: `Diatonic Groups Of Four`.

2. Descending thirds chain
   - Source basis: Trey discussed diatonic-thirds pattern variation.
   - Skill: composed-sounding melody from scale material.
   - App card: `Descending Thirds Chain`.

3. Chromatic minor-third cell
   - Source basis: Trey discussed a chromatic pattern built from minor-third and half-step movement.
   - Skill: outside vocabulary that still resolves.
   - App card: `Chromatic Minor-Third Cell`.

4. Diminished doorway
   - Source basis: Trey recommended transposing patterns into diminished environments.
   - Skill: symmetrical tension, then plain-spoken release.
   - App card: `Diminished Doorway`.

5. Arpeggio spine
   - Source basis: harmony-first composition and composed Phish epics.
   - Skill: chord tones as the skeleton, scale tones as connective tissue.
   - App card: `Arpeggio Spine`.

6. Mixolydian/Dorian pivot
   - Source basis: modal pattern movement and Phish one-chord jam vocabulary.
   - Skill: color control around major/minor third and b7.
   - App card: `Mixolydian/Dorian Pivot`.

7. Stash tension/release
   - Source basis: Stash song history explicitly centers tension/release.
   - Skill: staying tense long enough, then resolving clearly.
   - App card: `Stash Tension/Release`.

8. Divided Sky sustain
   - Source basis: melody, pause, and release-note behavior in Divided Sky.
   - Skill: silence, vibrato, patience, note endings.
   - App card: `Divided Sky Sustain`.

9. Reba peak ladder
   - Source basis: Reba's patient jam ascent and release.
   - Skill: building a solo toward one high point instead of overplaying early.
   - App card: `Reba Peak Ladder`.

## Six-Week Curriculum

Week 1: Diatonic Machinery
- Goal: groups of four and thirds in two positions.
- Pass: three keys at 90 bpm, starts from multiple scale degrees, one clean 60-second recorded chorus.

Week 2: Harmony First
- Goal: arpeggios as the skeleton.
- Pass: name chord tones while playing, resolve phrases to 3/5/7, leave counted silence.

Week 3: Controlled Dissonance
- Goal: chromatic and diminished tension that resolves.
- Pass: outside phrases resolve on beat one, diminished cells in four roots, release is obvious on recording.

Week 4: Modal Color
- Goal: major/minor third pivot over b7-centered grooves.
- Pass: switch 3 and b3 without tempo drift, preserve a rhythmic motif, reduce note density in the second half.

Week 5: Peak And Release
- Goal: Reba/Divided Sky-style patience and sustain.
- Pass: controlled vibrato for four beats, one clear peak in 90 seconds, counted silence.

Week 6: Integration
- Goal: choose devices musically under practice-room pressure.
- Pass: five vocabulary cards in one session, identify devices in your own recording, write one original lick per device.

## Tone Practice Notes

- VERIFIED: Keep both Tube Screamers available. Use one low-gain stage and one stronger stage to practice stacked sustain without smearing articulation.
- VERIFIED: Compression can help expose clean run consistency, but should not be used to hide weak fretting.
- INFERRED: For the first six weeks, practice mostly dry or lightly driven. Add Whammy, wah, vibe, delay, and looping only after the vocabulary stays in time without effects.
